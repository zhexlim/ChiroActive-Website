Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Introduction

ChiroActive ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, store, and disclose personal information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs).

By using our services or visiting our website, you consent to the collection and use of your information as described in this policy.

What information we collect

We collect personal information necessary to provide chiropractic services and manage your healthcare. This may include:

  • Full name, date of birth, and contact details (address, phone number, email)
  • Medicare number and private health insurance details
  • Medical history, current medications, and health-related information
  • Referral information from other healthcare providers
  • Payment and billing information
  • Notes from clinical assessments and treatment sessions

We collect this information directly from you at the time of your appointment, through intake forms, or during consultations. We may also receive information from referring practitioners with your consent.

How we use your information

We use your personal information to:

  • Provide, manage, and coordinate your chiropractic care
  • Communicate with you about appointments, follow-ups, and your health
  • Process health fund claims and billing through HICAPS
  • Comply with our legal and professional obligations as an AHPRA-registered practitioner
  • Improve the quality and safety of our services

We will not use your personal information for marketing purposes without your explicit consent.

Disclosure of your information

We may share your personal information with third parties only where necessary, including:

  • Other healthcare providers involved in your care (with your consent)
  • Health funds and insurers for the purpose of processing claims
  • AHPRA and regulatory bodies as required by law
  • Emergency services in situations involving risk to life

We do not sell or share your personal information with third parties for commercial purposes.

Storage and security

Your personal information is stored securely in our clinical management system. We take reasonable steps to protect your information from unauthorised access, disclosure, or misuse.

Patient health records are retained in accordance with state and federal legislative requirements. In South Australia, health records are generally retained for a minimum of 7 years from the last date of service (or until the patient turns 25 if a minor).

Accessing and correcting your information

You have the right to request access to the personal information we hold about you, and to request corrections if it is inaccurate or out of date. To make a request, please contact us using the details below.

We will respond to access or correction requests within 30 days. There may be circumstances in which we are unable to provide access — for example, where providing access would be unlawful. In such cases, we will advise you of the reason.

Cookies and website data

Our website does not currently use tracking cookies or analytics software that collects personally identifiable information. Standard web server logs (IP addresses, page requests) may be retained temporarily for security and diagnostic purposes.

Contact us
