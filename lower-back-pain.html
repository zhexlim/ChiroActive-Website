Condition

Lower Back Pain & Sciatica

One of the most common reasons people visit a chiropractor — and one we have extensive experience treating.

Why lower back pain is so debilitating

Ongoing lower back pain or sciatica can make simple tasks — standing, sitting, bending, sleeping — genuinely difficult. It affects how you work, move, and live day to day.

The causes are varied: disc problems, joint irritation, muscle tension, nerve compression. Getting the right diagnosis is the first step to effective treatment.

How we approach it

We begin with a thorough assessment to understand what's actually going on — not just where it hurts, but why. From there, treatment may include manual adjustments, flexion distraction (a gentle, low-force technique particularly effective for disc problems and sciatica), soft tissue work, and rehabilitation advice.

We'll explain exactly what we found and what we recommend before we start — and we'll check in regularly to make sure the treatment is working.

Common symptoms

Persistent aching or stiffness in the lower back
Sharp or shooting pain into the buttock or leg (sciatica)
Pain that worsens with sitting or standing for long periods
Difficulty bending, lifting, or changing position
Numbness or tingling in the legs or feet
Pain that disrupts sleep
